A Study About the Relation Among USD/TRY Exchange Rates, Foreign Debt Amount (in million USD) and Residential Property Price Index (RPPI)

1. Introduction

In brief, the relationships between USD/TRY Exchange Rates, Foreign Debt Amount (in million USD) and Residential Property Price Index (RPPI) are examined in this study among the years 2013 - 2020 in monthly scale starting from January until December through data visualization, corelogram and Pearson’s product-moment correlation.

These topics are chosen exceptionally. Why I chosen these topics can be explained by Republic of Turkey’s debts are generally foreign debts, i.e. borrowed in foreign currency, and this condition have been affected and have affected another economical values. To clarify, Turkish government have continued to borrow USD as a debt for as long as I’ve known. Also, USD against Turkish Lira exchange rate (USD/TL) is the foremost factor determining the residential prices due to the prices of iron are determined in USD globally, not in Turkish Lira (TL). Furthermore, residential properties are constructed with local materials alongwith imported materials in considerable amount. Therefore, USD/TL rate affects the foreign debt amount and RPPI.

Besides, borrowing more foreign debts in USD ends up again increasing value of USD against TL. Accordingly, there is at least one vicious circle among these topics. Therefore, it can be asserted that USD/TL exchange rate affects foreign debt amount and RPPI. Yet, how much are we sure about their relationships? Can we say that the same condition holds for vice versa?

If we want to reach a conclusion, we need to examine our data visually and statistically, i.e. mathematically. Therefore, I’m going to start with showing the histogram of our data. foreign debt amount, and RPPI are shown below, respectively:

3. Conclusion

As a consequence, the relationships between USD/TRY Exchange Rates, Foreign Debt Amount (in million USD) and Residential Property Price Index (RPPI) are examined in this study among the years 2013 - 2020 in monthly scale starting from January until December through data visualization, corelogram and Pearson’s product-moment correlation. Then, it was found that USD/TRY Exchange Rates and Residential Property Price Index (RPPI) are found to be correlated with each other, seen from both the corelogram and the Pearson’s product-moment correlation calculations both with same results, deduced from having large t-value and small p-value only at the USD/TL exchange rate and RPPI’s correlation calculation. Some of the specific years’ data are found to be correlated in itself;however, this situation supposably resulted from the data amount, i.e. if we have more data then we might confidently asserted that these data were related with each other.
